This tool uses basic information on smoking behavior and data from the NCI Prostate Lung Colorectal and Ovarian Cancer Screening Trial (PLCO) to predict the user’s chance of being diagnosed with lung cancer in the next 5 years, or dying from lung cancer in the next 5 years.

The Risk-based NLST Outcome Tool (RNOT) estimates the user’s chance of dying from lung cancer (using the Lung Cancer Death Risk Assessment Tool (LCDRAT)).

The best way to lower your risk of lung cancer, and all smoking-related diseases, is to quit smoking. Learn more by visiting smokefree.gov, the CDC quitting smoking page, or by calling 1-800-QUIT-NOW.
